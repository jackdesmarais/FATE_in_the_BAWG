# Curvature
The realm of bent lines, warped space, gravity, and non-Euclidean geometry.

## Truths
1. **Curvature** changes angles and bends lines
2. **Curvature** in motion is acceleration
3. **Curvature** in the universe is gravity
4. **Curvature** alters distances
5. **Curvature** is not straight
6. **Curvature** changes how big space is

## Approaches
1. Warpingly
2. Acceleratingly
3. Gravitationally
4. Non-Euclidianly
5. Queerly
6. House-of-Leaves-ishly

## Channeling examples
1. Create an advantage
	1. Curve a door so it gets stuck in a jam
	2. Curve space so a staircase Eschers
	3. Curve the universe to make gravity stronger or point in a new direction (as long as it still points down too)
2. Overcome
	1. Curve a door so it doesn't fit the jam and pops open
	2. Curve cell bars so you can exit a jail cell
	3. Curve space so you can step over a crevice or up onto a balcony
3. Attack
	1. Curve someone's limbs to take them out of a fight
	2. Curve the motion of an object to accelerate it into an enemy
	3. Curve the universe to crush someone with extra gravity
4. Defend
	1. Curve a weapon so it misses you
	2. Curve distance so a weapon misses you

## Adaptations
1. **Elf sight:** You can see anything where you could draw an unoccluded path, as if that path was straight. You have 360° vision, your vision is not blocked by the horizon, and you can see what is on the other side of a door through the gap at the jam. This does not magnify or illuminate anything, and things seen on very convoluted paths are very small.
2. **Instinctive geodesic compression:** You reduce the scale of any distance you are trying to travel by 1. This only works when you are directly controlling the movement and it causes fisheye visual and crazy air pressure/wind disturbances as scene aspects when used.
3. **A knack for spatial reasoning:** You can always find a bit of extra space when trying to fit something into a small space. Treat small spaces as having +1 scale when fitting things into them (this doesn't make them any less heavy).

## Attunement
1. **Sense:** You can sense nearby curved things and have an intrinsic sense of their curvature, including gravitational force, nearby acceleration, and non-straightness.
2. **Control:** Change the curvature direction of already-curved objects, change the direction of gravity or an object's acceleration, reshape a room (without changing volume), or redistribute the angles of an object.
3. **Transmute:** Make a curved object straight but very heavy; make an accelerating object stop and become warped; turn weight into acceleration to throw very heavy objects.
4. **Redefine:** Curvature sublimates off as a mist over time, turning things straight and floaty, then rains down elsewhere making things super heavy and bent.
