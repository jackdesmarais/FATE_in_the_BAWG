# Karst
A realm of underground caves and limestone rock hollowed out by seeping water. Important for [The Depths](/FATE_in_the_BAWG/locations/The_Depths.html), and currently manifested in the bottom floors of [Pulley tower](/FATE_in_the_BAWG/locations/Pulley_tower.html) through the avatar [Karst](/FATE_in_the_BAWG/NPCs/Karst.html).

## Aspects
**Karst is the water-carved depths** \
**It is so simple to get lost or trapped** \
**Eroded by time in the darkest depths** \
**Beware bad air, rockfalls, and sinkholes**

## Truths
1. **Karst** is subterranean stone shaped by water
2. **Karst** is labyrinthine tunnels
3. **Karst** forms in long-buried bones and shells
4. **Karst** rotted stone collapses suddenly
5. **Karst** rests in darkness
6. **Karst** is prone to squeezes, dead air, and floods

## Approaches
1. Erosively
2. Labyrinthinely
3. Diluvianly
4. Implosively
5. Darkly
6. Spelunk-hazardously

## Channeling examples
1. Create an advantage
	1. Create a sinkhole, rockfall, or squeeze to obstruct passage
	2. Make an area more maze-like or dark
2. Overcome
	1. Erode an obstacle
3. Attack
	1. Suffocate someone with bad air
	2. Drop them into a sinkhole
4. Defend
	1. Create darkness to disrupt an attacker
	2. Narrow the walls to disrupt an attack

## Adaptations
1. You may reshape wet stone.
2. You always remember your path in a maze. You don't necessarily know how to find something, but you can always find your way to somewhere you have been before, and know where that is in direction and distance from where you are now.

## Attunement
1. **Sense:** You can sense caves, underground water, rotten stone, and old bones.
2. **Control:** You may reshape caves, bring water from subterranean aquifers to where you are, and move cave hazards.
3. **Transmute:** You can make cave hazards in an existing cave, and may change the composition of the walls (as long as they remain a carbonate stone).
4. **Redefine:** Karst is now the digestive system of the earth. It pushes water, air, and occupants along with peristalsis. Watch out for stalactites and crushing narrow passages as the ripples pass.
