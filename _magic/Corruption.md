# Corruption
Corruption measures how much excess spiritual resonance a character has absorbed by using their magic at [Scale](/FATE_in_the_BAWG/subsystems/Scale.html) or by exposure to large concentrations of magical energy. Excess corruption can lead to various mutations, curses, reality breaches, or other strange afflictions, and large enough amounts can cause a character to fully breach into a [Spiritual realm](/FATE_in_the_BAWG/magic/Spiritual_realms.html), forming a permanent bridge and becoming an avatar of that realm. In some cases, however, exposure to corruption can lead to the development of [Adaptations](/FATE_in_the_BAWG/magic/Adaptations.html) or [Channeling](/FATE_in_the_BAWG/magic/Channeling.html).

## Implementation
Characters take an additional track (resonance). Sources of corruption increase this track. Each time corruption increases, roll grounding against your current corruption — if you succeed, everything is ok. Otherwise, reduce corruption by the amount you failed and absorb that amount with consequences. Consequences due to corruption reflect mutations, curses, reality breaches, or other strange afflictions. While these are generally bad, they frequently come with some sort of beneficial adaptation. If so, using this adaptation always costs a point of corruption.

Extreme consequences reflect beginning the process of becoming a spirit, replacing an aspect with one related to how you are physically becoming part of your element. (You can use this to claim some permissions, but until the aspect begins healing it will have serious negative drawbacks as well.) When this happens, replace a skill with a realm approach and you gain the ability to take [Attunement](/FATE_in_the_BAWG/magic/Attunement.html) — using any of these abilities costs a point of corruption. While a corruption consequence is marked as healing, you can reduce refresh by 1 to make the adaptation (or approach + attunement openness) permanent. Being taken out by corruption generally fully transforms you into a spirit or avatar.

In addition to taking consequences, corruption can be reduced by venting it into your environment. Each point vented creates a consequence on the scene representing the effects of resonance on the environment; these effects tend to be terrible for everyone. If you take out the scene, expect massive dimensional rifts, explosions, and negative aspects placed on the campaign permanently.
